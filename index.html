Butter Chicken

A rich, creamy, and deeply spiced homemade version

Ingredients

For the Marinade

  • 2.5 lbs boneless, skinless chicken thighs
  • 5 Tbsp minced ginger
  • 1 cup plain whole yogurt
  • 1 1/2 tsp turmeric
  • 1 1/2 tsp salt

For the Sauce

  • 4 Tbsp olive oil (mild taste)
  • 3 tsp ground cumin
  • 3 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1/4 tsp cinnamon
  • 4 Tbsp butter
  • 30 oz canned tomato sauce
  • 1/4 cup onion powder
  • 2 Tbsp freeze-dried garlic
  • 1/4 tsp degi mirch
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 cups half-and-half
  • 2 Tbsp sugar
  • 1 tsp kasoori methi
  • 1 Tbsp olive oil (for frying)

Instructions

  1. Chop the chicken into bite-sized pieces. Mince the ginger by slicing thin (cross grain) and finely chopping. Combine all marinade ingredients, mix well, cover, and refrigerate.
  2. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium-high heat. Add cumin, garam masala, coriander, and cinnamon. Toast for about 20 seconds until fragrant.
  3. Immediately add tomato sauce and butter. Stir with a flat-ended spatula. Mix in onion powder, freeze-dried garlic, degi mirch, and salt.
  4. Add half-and-half, sugar, and kasoori methi. In a separate cast iron pan, heat remaining olive oil and fry chicken in batches for about 3 minutes per side until browned.
  5. Add cooked chicken to the sauce. Simmer for 8–10 minutes until fully cooked through.
